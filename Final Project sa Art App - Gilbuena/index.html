Arts Showcase

Explore the beauty of Visual Arts, Performing Arts, and Literary Arts from the Philippines.

Visual Arts Performing Arts Literary Arts References

Visual Arts

1. Spoliarium

Creator: Juan Luna

An iconic painting depicting dying gladiators being dragged into a chamber. A masterpiece symbolizing the oppression of Filipinos during Spanish rule.

2. The Making of the Philippine Flag

Creator: Fernando Amorsolo

A classic painting showcasing women sewing the first Philippine flag, embodying nationalism and unity.

3. Mother and Child

Creator: Anita Magsaysay-Ho

A celebrated painting highlighting Filipino women and rural life, rendered in a modernist style.

4. Rice Planting

Creator: Fernando Amorsolo

A pastoral depiction of Filipino farmers working in the rice fields, showcasing the idyllic rural life.

5. San Sebastian Basilica

Architect: Genaro Palacios

The first all-steel building in Asia and the only prefabricated steel church in the world, located in Manila.

6. Bulul

Creator: Ifugao Artisans

Wooden sculptures representing ancestral spirits, essential in Ifugao rice rituals.

7. The Oblation

Creator: Guillermo Tolentino

A statue symbolizing selfless dedication, prominently displayed at the University of the Philippines.

8. Sabel

Creator: Benedicto Cabrera (BenCab)

An abstract portrayal of a scavenger woman, turning her into a symbol of resilience and strength.

9. Tinikling Bamboo Art

Creator: Filipino Artisans

Decorative representations inspired by the Tinikling dance, featuring intricate bamboo carvings.

10. Manunggul Jar

Creator: Prehistoric Filipino Artisans

A burial jar featuring intricate designs, signifying early Filipino artistry and beliefs about the afterlife.

Performing Arts

1. Singkil

Performer/Artist: Maranao People

A traditional Filipino dance that showcases grace and agility while stepping between bamboo poles.

2. Lea Salonga in Miss Saigon

Performer/Artist: Lea Salonga

An internationally acclaimed performance by Lea Salonga that earned her a Tony Award for her role as Kim.

3. Bayanihan Dance Company

Group: Bayanihan Philippine National Folk Dance Company

Renowned for their stunning performances that celebrate Filipino culture through traditional dance.

4. Ang Larawan (The Portrait)

Creator: Rolando Tinio (playwright)

A musical adaptation of Nick Joaquin’s "A Portrait of the Artist as Filipino."

5. PETA’s Rak of Aegis

Group: Philippine Educational Theater Association

A musical featuring the music of Aegis, highlighting Filipino resilience in the face of adversity.

6. Tinikling Dance

Performer: Filipino Folk Dancers

A traditional dance mimicking the movement of tikling birds, performed with bamboo poles.

7. Kundiman

Artist: Various Filipino Composers

A genre of traditional Filipino love songs, expressing deep emotion and patriotism.

8. The Ramon Obusan Folkloric Group

Group: Ramon Obusan Folkloric Group

A celebrated group known for preserving and performing authentic Filipino traditional dances.

9. Gawad CCP Para sa Sining

Event: Cultural Center of the Philippines

An annual event honoring Filipino artists for their contributions to the performing arts.

10. Philippine Madrigal Singers

Group: Philippine Madrigal Singers

A world-renowned choir known for their diverse repertoire and unique performance style.

Literary Arts

1. Noli Me Tangere

Author: Dr. José Rizal

A novel exposing the abuses of Spanish colonizers in the Philippines, inspiring national revolution.

2. Florante at Laura

Author: Francisco Balagtas

A metrical romance that combines themes of love, betrayal, and patriotism in a poetic narrative.

3. Mi Ultimo Adios

Author: Dr. José Rizal

A poignant farewell poem written by Rizal before his execution, expressing love for the Philippines.

4. Banaag at Sikat

Author: Lope K. Santos

A pioneering work in Filipino literature tackling social justice and labor rights.

5. Sa Aking Mga Kabata

Author: Dr. José Rizal

A poem encouraging Filipinos to embrace and cherish their native language.

6. Dekada '70

Author: Lualhati Bautista

A novel depicting the struggles of a Filipino family during Martial Law, highlighting human rights issues.

7. El Filibusterismo

Author: Dr. José Rizal

The sequel to Noli Me Tangere, delving deeper into themes of social reform and revolution.

8. Mga Ibong Mandaragit

Author: Amado V. Hernandez

A social realist novel addressing issues of oppression, exploitation, and social change in the Philippines.

9. Tundo: Man May Langit Din

Author: Andres Cristobal Cruz

A story of ambition, love, and social inequity set in Tondo, Manila.

10. Ang Bayan Ko

Author: Jose Corazon de Jesus

A patriotic poem reflecting the Filipino spirit and longing for independence.
